aboutabout Jonathan

I enjoy turning complex problems into simple, beautiful and intuitive designs. When I'm not coding or designing apps, you'll find me in Nature on a trail with my cattledog - Harley, trying out a new restaurant, discovering live local music or writing & illustrating my next children's book.

With more than a decade of experience I tackle a broad variety of disciplines in the process of creating meaningful products that make a real difference. With carefully selected tools and skills I express my visions ranging from product design to the creation of user experience up to development and delivery.

My distinctive strategic thinking helps me to pinpoint the holistic scope of a product and evolve it by adding new business ideas, innovations and values that meet user needs as well as business objectives. Besides building user flows, information architecture and wireframes, tailor-made interactive prototypes are constantly putting concepts and ideas to the test and emphasize the user-centered approach in my work. I work with the latest techniques in web development and my agile workflow is the result of the precisely combined tools and strategies that include object oriented patterns, build scripts, pre-processors and minification, source code management, testing as well as deployment.

I like to think of myself as a tech-oriented human being. I understand technology but technology by itself isn't the thing that excites me, instead, the results that can be achieved with technology are. When discussing new ideas, I always try to think about the goals first and the means to get there second.

Project: Eye Spy

Links


Team

S. Benedict, J. Gallo, G. Blackmon, and R. Dang

Role

Front End Developer (JavaScript / jQuery), Web Design, Logo and Graphics (Adobe Illustrator and PhotoShop)

Description

Eye Spy is a scavenger hunt game that has the user upload an image based off the clues given from an AI called Clarifai. This AI is used to analyze both the uploaded image and the random image that is pulled from Flickr and gives back several descriptions about the pictures. The player is scored based off how similar the image they uploaded is to the Flickr photo.

This application was developed during the course of a 2 day hackathon. The technologies utilized in this project were: JavaScript/jQuery, Bootstrap3, and HTML. AJAX calls were made to Clarifai, and Flickr to enchance the user experience.

Project: The World of Oneiro

Description

The World of Oneiro is a large scope solo project.

demonstrating my skills in Project Planning, Design tools, soft skills and programming.

Links

The World of Oneiro first took place as a Kickstart campaign to write and illustrate a children’s book as a first time Author and illustrator.

This project had a lot of first for me. I made the Oneiro website, filmed and edited the Kickstarter video, wrote copy, made a plan, ran FaceBook ads, social media campaigns.

The Kickstarter raised over $10,000 in pre-sales. Once it funded, I wrote an outline, made a cast of characters, wrote the story, made a storyboard, Illustrated the images. The first images where sketched on paper, the backgrounds were done with watercolor and acrylic paint. Then brought onto the computer in illustrator and photoshop. Edits made, with the help and feedback from friends, family and kickstarter backers.

The Book was published in 2018. I visited local schools and did reading, talks, Q&A’s with the kids. It was a fun and amazing experience.

Just a few weeks ago I made an animated flipbook of The World of Oneiro. The demonstrated my skills in JavaScript, jQuery, HTML, and CSS. I have plans for the Oneiro website 2.0 to have a kids corner with games and other entertaining / educational / inspiring resources. And a Parent/ Teacher Portal as a resources.
